PROJECT PROPOSAL
Title: SmartPost: A Digital Academic Postponement Management System for IAA
College

1.1 Problem Statement
At IAA College, students currently rely on paper-based forms to reqsuest
postponement of
academic years or semesters. This manual process is prone to inefficiencies such as
misplaced
forms, delays in approvals, lack of transparency, and difficulty in tracking records.
Students often
face challenges in attaching evidence, following up on approvals, and ensuring
compliance with
regulations. Administrators also struggle with managing large volumes of paper
records, verifying
authenticity, and producing timely reports. There is therefore a need for a digital
system that
automates, secures, and streamlines the postponement request process.

1.2 Main Objective
To develop SmartPost System: A Digital Academic Postponement Management
System for IAA Collage

1.3 Specific Objectives and Processes
i.

To develop user (students, staff and admin) management subsystem.

ii.

To develop postponement and approval subsystem.

iii.

To develop verification subsystem for student details and payment status.

iv.

To develop document Management Subsystem.

v.

To develop Reporting and Analytics Subsystem.

1.4 Significance of the Project
A. For Students: Simplifies the postponement process, reduces delays, and
ensures
transparency.

B. For Academic Staff: Streamlines approvals, reduces paperwork, and improves
efficiency.
C. For IAA College: Demonstrates innovation in academic administration,
aligning with
digital transformation goal.
D. For Academia: Serves as a case study in digitizing administrative workflows.
E. For Society: Encourages trust in digital systems and prepares students for egovernance
practices.

1.5 Scope of the Project
1. Connectivity Dependency – the system requires stable internet/network
access no offline mode is available
2. Integration Constraints – Real-time sync with IAA’s existing student
database may not be fully archivable if it lacks a proper API.
3. User adoption – some staff and student may resist the shift from paper-based
processes, requiring training.
4. Document Authenticity – the system cannot verify if uploaded evidence (eg.,
medical reports) is genuine; human review is still needed.
5. Single campus Scope – SmartPost is designed for IAA College only, limiting
broader applicability.
6. Policy Dependency – Changes in IAA’s academic regulations require manual
system updates, the system cannot auto-adapt
7. Data Security Reliance – Overall security depends on the hosting
environment, which is outside the system’s direct control.
